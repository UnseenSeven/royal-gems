Aquamarine

Believed to protect sailors and guarantee safe voyages, these gems have a fascinating blue hue.

Gemology Facts

Aquamarine is a variety of beryl. Its name comes from the Latin for seawater, and it ranges in color from greenish blue to blue.

Historic Significance

Aquamarine was used by sailors as a talisman of good luck, fearlessness, and protection. It was also believed to be the treasure of mermaids.
